Get Started with Staking

Written by David August.

What is "Staking?": Proof-of-stake (PoS) is an alternative consensus algorithm to Bitcoin’s proof-of-work (PoW). Unlike mining, which requires massive electrical power to validate transactions, staking is a more eco-friendly process.


How To Stake your Crypto: A software wallet is essential to the staking process as it is where you store the funds used for staking. Simply go to the website of the coin you want to stake and download the wallet. I personally use Exodus because it is powerful and simple to use.


Determine the coins requirements: Some PoS networks have a minimum number of coins required in order to stake. Tezos requires 10,000 XTZ, while Ethereum plans to start with 32 ETH. If you are starting with a small amount of money, you can stake any amount of ATOM or ADA.


Start Staking! Once you choose the coins you would like to stake, make sure you're connected to the internet (try to be connected as much as possible, shoot for 24/7) and start staking! You will be earning a small percentage of your total balance every day.


Popular coins to stake: Tezos (XTZ), Cosmos (ATOM), Cardano (ADA), Polkadot (DOT), Synthetix Network (SNX), Dash (DASH), Neo (NEO), Ontology (ONT), and Ethereum (ETH) is coming soon.
